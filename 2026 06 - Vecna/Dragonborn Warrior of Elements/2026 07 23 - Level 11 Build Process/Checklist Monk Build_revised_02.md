# Sritooh Triithe — Level 11 Build: Essential vs. Advanced (Revised 02)

## Use this first: the under-two-hour plan

You do **not** need an optimized or macro-heavy character to be ready. Your minimum successful build is a correct level-11 sheet plus one clear reminder for Elemental Attunement and flight. Finish the **Essential build** before touching the **Advanced build**.

| Time | Do this | Done means |
|---:|---|---|
| 0–15 min | Ask the three P0 questions below | You know HP method, Resilient choice, and any campaign rule exception. |
| 15–40 min | Level the Roll20 sheet and correct actions | All values in the Essential sheet checklist are correct. |
| 40–55 min | Set two markers and test one roll of each main action | You can find Attunement, flight altitude, Focus, and attacks without hunting. |
| 55–70 min | Make a one-screen combat note | You know the turn-start, normal turn, and emergency turn. |
| 70–120 min | Optional only | Add quick rolls, ask about advanced tactics, or polish token/macro setup. Stop when time runs out. |

If you have only 30 minutes, do the P0 questions, the **must-change values**, and two markers. Everything else can wait until after the session.

## What the Gemini notes add—and how much to rely on it

The two contextualization notes reinforce the core 2024 interpretation: Elemental Attunement is a 1-Focus, start-of-turn feature; it improves Unarmed Strikes; and Stride of the Elements makes Sritooh a 50-foot flyer/swimmer while Attuned. Their useful tactical theme is **battlefield control**, especially moving targets through an ally's hazard rather than trying to become a spellcaster.

Treat these claims carefully:

| Gemini advice | Use now? | Revised guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Stay at 15-foot elemental Unarmed Strike reach and push/pull around hazards. | **Yes—essential tactic.** | It is simple, useful, and needs no new feat. Coordinate with party hazards when they naturally appear; do not demand a party rebuild around it. |
| Use Thunder as the default elemental type. | **Sometimes.** | Thunder is a reasonable starting choice, but choose a type based on the current enemy, story clues, and a resistance you know about. Use Force when reliable damage matters more than forced movement. |
| Ignore Elemental Burst entirely. | **No.** | It is not the default single-target turn, but keep it as a 2-Focus, 120-foot, 20-foot-radius option for clustered enemies, unreachable targets, or when multiple enemies are near defeat. |
| Fly above an enemy, pull them upward, then use the fall/Prone result. | **Advanced / DM approval.** | It can be effective, but it needs a ruling on vertical forced movement and map handling. Do not make it your first-session plan. |
| Plan around Grappler / 15-foot grapples. | **Advanced / not current.** | Sritooh does not have Grappler. The reach interaction for grapples is a real table question, not a prerequisite for this level. |
| Obtain Wraps of Unarmed Power. | **Good future request.** | Ask about them after the essential sheet works; do not add them to inventory without a DM award or permission. |
| Uncanny Metabolism regains Focus based on a Martial Arts roll. | **Incorrect—do not use.** | Per the captured level-10 sheet, it restores **all expended Focus** and heals `Monk level + Martial Arts die` when initiative is rolled, once per Long Rest: at level 11, `11 + 1d10`. |

## Essential build — finish this before the session

### Three questions to send or ask the DM now (P0)

1. **“For this level-up, do I take fixed HP or roll, and does the level-up take effect before the next session?”** With CON +2, fixed HP makes Sritooh's maximum HP **80** (73 + 7). Do not assume the current 53 HP carries forward; the source sheet is a mid-fight snapshot.
2. **“Which ability did my Resilient feat increase and give saving-throw proficiency?”** The sheet shows the feat but no selected ability or extra save proficiency. Constitution is plausible, but not confirmed.
3. **“Are we using the 2024 rules as shown in Roll20, with any house rules I should enter on my sheet?”** This prevents a legacy card from being mistaken for the campaign rule.

Do these manually or verify them after using Roll20's level-up flow.

- [ ] Monk level: **11**; Hit Dice: **11d8**.
- [ ] Maximum Focus: **11**. Current Focus is whatever the actual encounter/rest state is, not automatically 11.
- [ ] Martial Arts die: **1d10**.
- [ ] Unarmed Strike / Bonus Unarmed Strike: `1d10 + 3`.
- [ ] Flurry of Blows: three Unarmed Strikes, each `1d10 + 3`, for 1 Focus.
- [ ] Elemental Burst: `3d10`; 2 Focus; Magic action; DC 15 Dexterity save.
- [ ] Focused Patient Defense temporary HP: `2d10`.
- [ ] Uncanny Metabolism healing: `11 + 1d10`; once per Long Rest; restores all expended Focus.
- [ ] Deflect Attacks reduction: `1d10 + 14`.
- [ ] Redirect Attack damage, if entered separately: `2d10 + 3`.
- [ ] Slow Fall reduction: **55**.
- [ ] Breath Weapon (both line and cone): **3d10 Acid**, DC 14, four uses per Long Rest. The old 1d10 display was already incorrect at level 10.

### Add the one new level-11 feature correctly

- [ ] Add **Stride of the Elements**: *while Elemental Attunement is active*, Fly Speed 50 ft and Swim Speed 50 ft.
- [ ] Keep ordinary Speed at 50 ft. Do **not** set an always-on Fly Speed merely because Sritooh is level 11; flight is conditional on Attunement.
- [ ] Keep Draconic Flight separate: it is a 50-foot Fly Speed for 10 minutes, 1/Long Rest. It can be a fallback if Attunement is not active.
- [ ] Keep Elemental Attunement's start-of-turn cost at 1 Focus, 10-minute duration, 15-foot Unarmed Strike reach, elemental damage option, and STR 15 push/pull reminder.

### Two Roll20 markers—enough for a novice build

- [ ] **Attuned:** means “1 Focus spent; 10 minutes; 15-foot unarmed reach; elemental damage/control; Stride is on.”
- [ ] **Airborne @ __ ft:** record altitude in its label, token name, or GM-approved map note. Erase it when Sritooh lands.

Do not spend limited time making intricate macros. Roll the standard sheet attack, then say in chat: **“Elemental hit: STR DC 15 or move 10 ft toward/away.”** This is more reliable than a broken macro when you are new to Roll20.

## Essential combat note (copy into Roll20 bio or a handout)

**At the start of a meaningful fight:** Spend 1 Focus for Elemental Attunement; mark Attuned. This costs neither Action nor Bonus Action.

**Normal turn:** Move to a safe angle; make Unarmed Strikes from up to 15 feet; use elemental damage when the push/pull matters; spend 1 Focus on Flurry only when three extra strikes are valuable. Use Stunning Strike on an important hit, not every target.

**If flying:** stay low enough to remain part of the fight and record altitude. A flying creature without Hover falls if it is Incapacitated or Prone, or if its Fly Speed becomes 0. Slow Fall reduces 55 damage; it does not stop a fall.

**If enemies cluster or cannot be reached:** consider Elemental Burst. Do not delete or ignore it merely because it is less efficient than punching one target.

## Advanced build — useful later, not required today

### 1. Controlled “cheese-grater” positioning

With an allied persistent hazard—such as Web, a wall, a dangerous terrain feature, or another effect that penalizes movement—use elemental hits to push/pull a target into or through the bad space. This is powerful only when all of these are true:

- the enemy fails the STR save;
- the effect actually harms or restricts the enemy when moved there;
- the party has agreed not to block each other's lines and areas; and
- moving the enemy does not put an ally in more danger.

It is teamwork, not an automatic damage engine. Begin by asking allies, “Do you have a zone you want me to keep enemies in?”

### 2. Vertical pull / drop tactic

The Gemini notes suggest flying directly above a grounded enemy, pulling it upward 10 feet, allowing it to fall for 1d6 damage and the Prone condition, then closing to within 5 feet for follow-up attacks. This is an appealing advanced tactic, but it depends on a DM ruling about three-dimensional “toward/away” movement and Roll20 altitude. Use it only after asking:

> “If I am directly above a creature and it fails Elemental Attunement's STR save, may I pull it 10 feet vertically? If so, do you resolve the resulting 10-foot fall normally, including Prone?”

Do not argue the point mid-combat. If the answer is no or uncertain, use horizontal control instead.

### 3. Grapples at reach and aerial carrying

The 2024 Monk can use Dexterity for a grapple attempt, but the 15-foot reach is worded specifically around making an Unarmed Strike. The Gemini notes rightly identify an ambiguity about holding a grapple at that distance. Sritooh does not have Grappler, so this is a later specialization, not today's build task. Ask before attempting it:

> “Does Elemental Attunement's 15-foot reach apply to an Unarmed Strike that grapples and to maintaining that grapple? If I fly while grappling, how do you handle speed, size, altitude, and falling?”

### 4. Future item / feat conversation

After the sheet is correct, a simple DM-facing wishlist is enough:

- **Wraps of Unarmed Power** or another DM-approved unarmed-strike bonus: an advanced upgrade that directly supports the build; do not assume it is available.
- **Grappler** at a future feat level: consider only if the DM resolves reach-grapple handling favorably and you enjoy the control playstyle.
- **Dexterity, then Wisdom:** Sritooh currently has DEX 17 and WIS 16. A future ability-score decision should consider attack/damage/AC/initiative against save DC/control; it is a level-12 decision, not a level-11 emergency.

## What not to do under time pressure

- Do not multiclass, replace existing spells, invent an item, or add a feat at level 11.
- Do not replace all combat with Elemental Burst or delete it from the sheet.
- Do not assume a 15-foot grapple, vertical pull, ally airlift, or forced-movement combo works without the DM's answer.
- Do not overwrite temporary HP, current HP, Focus, or used-resource pips based solely on the July 23 screenshot.
- Do not spend the last hour debugging automation. A legible reminder and a manual roll are a successful Roll20 setup.
