# Checklist Monk Build

# Prompt
What should I do for my build from level 10 to level 11 monk warrior of the elements? What questions do I need to ask myself? Do I need to identify new spells, equipment? Create a checklist for me? Note I’m using roll20 online tabletop

# Response GPT 5.5 High

For **level 10 → level 11 Warrior of the Elements Monk**, your upgrade is mostly **mobility + better dice**, not spell selection. You do **not** need to pick new spells at level 11. Your subclass’s spell-like utility, **Elementalism**, came online at level 3, and level 11 gives **Stride of the Elements** instead. On Roll20, the important work is making sure your sheet, macros, resources, and token markers reflect the new movement options.

## What changes at Monk 11

At level 11, your Monk table changes to:

| Item               | Level 10 |                                     Level 11 |
| ------------------ | -------: | -------------------------------------------: |
| Proficiency Bonus  |       +4 |                                           +4 |
| Martial Arts die   |      1d8 |                                     **1d10** |
| Focus Points       |       10 |                                       **11** |
| Unarmored Movement |  +20 ft. |                                      +20 ft. |
| New feature        |        — | **Subclass feature: Stride of the Elements** |

Roll20’s 2024 Monk table lists level 11 as a subclass-feature level, with **1d10 Martial Arts**, **11 Focus Points**, and **+20 ft. Unarmored Movement**. ([Roll20][1])

Your Warrior of the Elements feature at level 11 is:

> While your **Elemental Attunement** is active, you also have a **Fly Speed** and **Swim Speed** equal to your Speed. ([Roll20][2])

That means your core combat identity shifts from “mobile reach striker” to **mobile reach striker who can now fight in 3D**.

---

# Big answer: no, you do not pick new spells

You do **not** identify new Monk spells at level 11. Warrior of the Elements does not work like Eldritch Knight, Ranger, Druid, Wizard, or Sorcerer spell progression.

You already know **Elementalism** from level 3, using Wisdom as your spellcasting ability. ([Roll20][2]) At level 11, you gain **flight and swim speed while Elemental Attunement is active**, not a spell list expansion. ([Roll20][2])

So your level-up checklist is about:

* Dice changes
* Focus Points
* HP
* Movement modes
* Roll20 attacks/macros
* Tactics
* DM rulings for flight, altitude, grappling, and maps

---

# Level 11 build questions to ask yourself

## 1. Am I using Elemental Attunement almost every fight?

At level 11, the value of Elemental Attunement increases sharply because it now enables your **fly speed** and **swim speed**. Elemental Attunement costs 1 Focus Point, starts at the beginning of your turn, lasts 10 minutes, and empowers your Unarmed Strikes with reach, elemental damage, and push/pull control. ([Roll20][2])

Ask yourself:

* Do I usually activate Elemental Attunement early?
* Am I saving 1 Focus Point for it before spending heavily on Flurry of Blows, Stunning Strike, or Elemental Burst?
* Am I tracking the 10-minute duration?
* Do I have a Roll20 token marker for “Attuned”?

My recommendation: **treat Elemental Attunement as your default combat stance** unless the fight is clearly trivial.

---

## 2. How will I use flight without getting myself killed?

Your new feature is powerful, but flight creates new tactical risks.

Ask yourself:

* Am I flying high enough to matter, or just hovering 5–15 feet above the battlefield?
* What happens if I become **Incapacitated** while Elemental Attunement is active?
* What happens if the 10-minute duration expires while I am airborne?
* Does my DM track altitude precisely?
* Can enemies still reach me with long reach, ranged attacks, flying, or spells?
* Do I have Slow Fall available if I drop?

Your **Slow Fall** also improves because it is based on Monk level. At level 11, it reduces falling damage by **55 damage** instead of 50. Roll20 lists Slow Fall as reducing fall damage by five times your Monk level. ([Roll20][1])

Practical tactic: do not automatically fly 60 feet up. Often, the sweet spot is **10–15 feet above or away from enemies**, where your extended elemental reach matters but you are not isolated from the party.

---

## 3. Am I built to exploit vertical grappling?

If you have the **Grappler** feat, level 11 is a major upgrade. The official D&D Beyond article specifically calls out that Grappler combines well with the Warrior of the Elements’ extended reach and the 2024 Monk’s ability to grapple using Dexterity. ([D&D Beyond][3])

Ask yourself:

* Do I have Grappler?
* Is my Dexterity high enough to make grappling reliable?
* Does my DM allow flying while dragging or carrying grappled creatures?
* How does your table handle weight, carrying capacity, and forced movement while flying?
* Can I pull enemies upward, drop them, or reposition them safely?

A strong level 11 pattern is:

1. Activate Elemental Attunement.
2. Fly or move into an angle enemies cannot easily answer.
3. Hit with Unarmed Strike.
4. Push or pull the enemy 10 feet.
5. Grapple if available.
6. Fly with the target only if your DM agrees the movement works that way.
7. Drop or isolate the target only if the table has clear falling and grappling rulings.

This is one of the main “ask your DM before combat” areas.

---

## 4. Am I updating all damage rolls from d8 to d10?

This is easy to miss on Roll20.

At level 11, your Martial Arts die becomes **1d10**. ([Roll20][1]) That affects more than your basic punch.

Update or verify:

* Unarmed Strike damage: **1d10 + Dexterity modifier**
* Bonus Action Unarmed Strike: **1d10 + Dexterity modifier**
* Flurry of Blows attacks: **1d10 + Dexterity modifier each**
* Elemental Burst: **3d10**, because it deals damage equal to three rolls of your Martial Arts die. ([Roll20][2])
* Patient Defense temporary HP: **2d10**, because Heightened Focus uses two rolls of your Martial Arts die when you spend Focus on Patient Defense. ([Roll20][1])
* Uncanny Metabolism healing: **11 + 1d10**, because it restores HP equal to your Monk level plus your Martial Arts die. ([Roll20][1])

Your actual attack bonus probably does **not** change at level 11 unless an item, buff, or ability score changed. Your Proficiency Bonus remains **+4**.

---

## 5. Am I spending Focus Points too aggressively?

You now have **11 Focus Points**, up from 10. ([Roll20][1]) But your good options are expensive:

* Elemental Attunement: 1 Focus Point
* Flurry of Blows: 1 Focus Point
* Stunning Strike: 1 Focus Point
* Elemental Burst: 2 Focus Points
* Patient Defense enhanced version: 1 Focus Point
* Step of the Wind enhanced movement utility: 1 Focus Point

At level 10, Heightened Focus improved Flurry of Blows, Patient Defense, and Step of the Wind. Flurry can now become **three Unarmed Strikes** when you spend Focus. Patient Defense can give temporary HP, and Step of the Wind can move a willing Large-or-smaller creature with you. ([Roll20][1])

Ask yourself:

* Do I spend Focus too early?
* Am I using Stunning Strike only when the target matters?
* Am I using Elemental Burst only when I can hit multiple enemies?
* Am I keeping 1 Focus Point available for mobility or defense?
* Do I remember Uncanny Metabolism can restore all Focus once per Long Rest when initiative is rolled?

# Equipment questions

You do not automatically get new equipment at level 11, but this is a good time to review your gear because your build now cares even more about **movement, defense, saves, and unarmed strike reliability**.

Ask your DM or check party loot for:

## Best equipment priorities

### 1. AC and saving throw protection

You are still a front-line or near-front-line character. Items like a **Ring of Protection** are very strong because they improve both AC and saving throws; Roll20 lists Ring of Protection as granting +1 AC and +1 to saving throws, requiring attunement. ([Roll20][4])

Good categories:

* +AC items
* +saving throw items
* defensive cloaks/rings
* Constitution-supporting items
* Wisdom-supporting items if your save DC matters often

### 2. Mobility enhancers

Since your fly and swim speeds equal your Speed, anything that increases your Speed also improves your level 11 feature.

Ask:

* Do I have Boots of Speed or another speed enhancer?
* Does my DM allow the item’s speed increase to apply to fly/swim speed from Stride of the Elements?
* Does Haste or another magical speed increase apply to my current Speed for this feature?

Confirm this with your DM before relying on it.

### 3. Unarmed strike support

Your Elemental Attunement only works with **Unarmed Strikes**, not Monk weapons. Roll20’s Warrior of the Elements entry says Elemental Attunement modifies your Unarmed Strikes with greater reach, elemental damage, and forced movement. ([Roll20][2])

Ask:

* Am I still using weapons when I should be using unarmed strikes?
* Do my items improve unarmed attacks specifically?
* Does my Roll20 attack entry correctly apply unarmed damage and elemental damage?

### 4. Ranged backup

You can use Elemental Burst at range, but it costs 2 Focus. It is a 20-foot-radius sphere within 120 feet and deals three Martial Arts dice of acid, cold, fire, lightning, or thunder damage, with a Dexterity save for half. ([Roll20][2])

Ask:

* Do I need a backup ranged weapon?
* Is Elemental Burst enough for flying or unreachable enemies?
* Can I afford to spend 2 Focus when I am already maintaining Attunement?

* [ ] Confirm Proficiency Bonus remains **+4**

* [ ] Confirm Focus Points changed from **10 → 11**

* [ ] Add or verify **Stride of the Elements**
* [ ] Confirm Elemental Attunement is listed and readable
* [ ] Confirm Elementalism is still available
* [ ] Confirm your save DC is correct: **8 + Wisdom modifier + Proficiency Bonus**
* [ ] Roll or assign new HP: **1d8 + Constitution modifier**, or fixed HP if your table uses average

## Attacks and actions

* [ ] Update Unarmed Strike damage to **1d10 + Dexterity modifier**
* [ ] Add damage-type note: acid, cold, fire, lightning, thunder, or normal damage
* [ ] Add note: while Elemental Attunement is active, Unarmed Strike reach is **10 feet greater than normal**
* [ ] Add forced movement note: Strength save or move target up to 10 feet toward/away from you
* [ ] Update Elemental Burst damage to **3d10**
* [ ] Update Patient Defense temp HP macro to **2d10**
* [ ] Update Uncanny Metabolism healing to **11 + 1d10**
* [ ] Update Slow Fall reminder to **55 damage reduction**
* [ ] Confirm Flurry of Blows macro rolls **three** Unarmed Strikes when spending Focus

## Resources

* [ ] Set Focus Points resource max to **11**
* [ ] Add a checkbox or note for **Uncanny Metabolism used / unused**
* [ ] Add a tracker for Elemental Attunement duration: **10 minutes**
* [ ] Add a reminder that Attunement ends if you are **Incapacitated**

# DM questions before your next session

These are the questions I would ask before combat starts:

1. **How do you want me to track flying altitude on Roll20?**

2. **If Elemental Attunement ends while I’m flying, do I immediately fall?**

3. **If I am Incapacitated while flying, do I fall immediately?**

4. **Can I fly while grappling a creature?**

5. **Can I drag, lift, or drop grappled enemies while flying?**

6. **How are carrying capacity and creature weight being handled?**

7. **Can I use my elemental push/pull vertically, or only horizontally?**

8. **Do flying enemies provoke opportunity attacks from me if they leave my extended reach?**

9. **Does my 10-foot additional reach make my effective unarmed reach 15 feet?**

10. **Do map obstacles still block line of sight or movement if I fly over them?**

11. **Can I end my turn in the air without hover?**

12. **Are we using 2024 rules consistently, or mixing 2014 and 2024 rules?**

That last question matters a lot in Roll20 because some compendium entries, magic items, monsters, or campaign defaults may still reflect older 2014 content unless the game is configured around 2024 sources.

# Tactical checklist for level 11 combat

## Start of combat

* [ ] Decide whether this fight is worth Elemental Attunement.
* [ ] If yes, spend 1 Focus at the start of your turn.
* [ ] Mark your token as **Attuned**.
* [ ] Choose your first elemental damage type based on enemy resistances or theme.
* [ ] Decide whether to fight from ground, wall, water, or air.

## Each turn

* [ ] Can I hit safely from extended reach?
* [ ] Can I fly to avoid melee retaliation?
* [ ] Can I push/pull an enemy into a hazard, ally’s area spell, or bad position?
* [ ] Is this target worth Stunning Strike?
* [ ] Is this a Flurry turn or a conserve-Focus turn?
* [ ] Can I use movement to rescue an ally with Step of the Wind?
* [ ] Am I ending my turn somewhere safe?
* [ ] Am I too far from the party?

## Use Elemental Burst when

* [ ] You can hit 3+ enemies.
* [ ] You can avoid hitting allies.
* [ ] Enemies are clustered.
* [ ] The enemy has poor Dexterity saves.
* [ ] You can choose a damage type they are unlikely to resist.
* [ ] You are not about to need those 2 Focus Points for survival.

## Use flight when

* [ ] The enemy has poor ranged options.
* [ ] The terrain has pits, walls, water, lava, cliffs, rooftops, ships, or vertical cover.
* [ ] You need to reach a caster, archer, flying enemy, switch, ledge, or objective.
* [ ] You can safely carry, drag, or reposition someone.
* [ ] You have a plan if Attunement ends.

# My recommendation for your level 11 playstyle

At level 11, stop thinking of your Monk as just a fast melee character. You are now a **three-dimensional battlefield controller**.

Your default loop should be:

1. **Activate Elemental Attunement.**
2. **Use unarmed strikes, not weapons.**
3. **Fight at extended reach.**
4. **Use push/pull every time positioning matters.**
5. **Use flight to choose angles enemies cannot answer.**
6. **Spend Focus on Flurry when the target matters.**
7. **Use Elemental Burst only when the enemy formation justifies the cost.**
8. **Track altitude clearly in Roll20.**

The biggest mechanical updates you need to make are: **Martial Arts die to 1d10, Focus Points to 11, Elemental Burst to 3d10, Patient Defense temp HP to 2d10, Uncanny Metabolism to 11 + 1d10, Slow Fall to 55, and add fly/swim speed while Elemental Attunement is active.**
